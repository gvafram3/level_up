⚠️

AI Can Be Wrong

AI tools can "hallucinate" — generate confident but incorrect information, especially for specific data, recent research, or technical details. Always verify critical facts against your textbook or lecturer. Use AI to understand and explore — not as a citation source.

Study Prompts — Works for Any Course

These prompt templates work regardless of your programme. Swap in your own course name, topic, or concept.

Concept Explanation"Explain [concept] to me in 3 stages: beginner, intermediate, then exam-level. After each stage, ask if I want to go deeper or be tested."
Active Recall Quiz"I am studying [topic] for my [course] exam. Ask me 5 exam-style questions one at a time. After each answer I give, tell me what I got right, what I missed, and the correct answer."
Past Question Practice"Here is a past exam question from my [course]: [paste question]. I will answer it now. After I answer, evaluate my response, give me a score out of 10, and tell me what a full-mark answer would include."
Comparison Table"Create a comparison table for [topic A] vs [topic B] vs [topic C]. Compare: definition, key features, examples, and common exam questions on each."
NotebookLM (after upload)"Based on the uploaded lecture notes, what are the 10 most exam-likely concepts? Create 5 short-answer questions from them and quiz me one at a time."
Weak Area Diagnosis"I scored poorly on [topic]. Ask me 5 diagnostic questions to find exactly where my understanding breaks down. Then explain only the gaps you find."
Day 3 — Practice Task

The AI Study Session

  • Choose one genuinely difficult topic from any of your courses
  • Ask Claude or ChatGPT to explain it three different ways (beginner / intermediate / exam-level)
  • Ask the AI to quiz you with 5 questions — answer before seeing feedback
  • Upload a lecture note to NotebookLM — ask it to generate 10 exam questions
  • Try one quiz platform (Quizizz or NotebookLM) — complete one study session
  • Write a 3-sentence summary of the topic entirely from memory, without notes
  • Look up KNUST's AI 150 course and note one thing it covers that you want to learn more about
