⚠️

AI Can Be Wrong

AI tools can "hallucinate" — generate confident but incorrect information, especially for specific drug dosages, recent research, or exact biochemical data. Always verify critical facts against your textbook. Use AI to understand and explore — not as a citation source.

Quiz & Practice Platforms

Upload Your Notes — Generate Practice Quizzes

These platforms let you submit course materials and generate interactive practice quizzes. This is a different level of exam preparation.

NotebookLM

notebooklm.google.com

Upload PDFs or notes. Ask it to generate quiz questions, test you on topics, or create a study guide — grounded in your own materials.

How: Upload lecture slides → "Create 10 MCQ on [topic] then quiz me one question at a time."
Try NotebookLM →

Quizizz

quizizz.com

Genuinely free quiz platform — no account needed to join a quiz. Works well on phones. Millions of ready-made quizzes including science topics.

How: Search your topic → Join or start a quiz → Play solo or with classmates in real time.
Try Quizizz →

Khan Academy

khanacademy.org

Free exercises and videos for chemistry, biology, maths, and more. Work through structured practice problems with instant feedback — no paid add-ons needed.

How: Create free account → Find your subject → Work through exercises and track your mastery.
Try Khan Academy →

Anki + AI Decks

apps.ankiweb.net

Spaced repetition app. Use Claude to generate Anki-format cards from your notes, then import. Best for memorisation-heavy content.

How: Prompt Claude: "Create 20 Anki flashcard pairs (front/back) on the TCA cycle." Import into Anki.
Try Anki →

Oppia

oppia.org

Free, open-source learning platform with community-built interactive lessons. Good for foundational science and maths concepts.

How: Browse explorations by topic → Work through interactive lessons that adapt to your answers.

Domain-Specific Prompts

BSc Human Nutrition & Dietetics

Year 1 — Sem 1 · NUTR / BIOL / CHEM
Macronutrient Understanding"Explain the roles of carbohydrates, proteins, and fats in human metabolism. Teach each one using real food examples. After each, ask me a question to check my understanding."
Clinical Case Practice"Give me a patient case showing signs of iron-deficiency anaemia. Describe the symptoms. Ask me to identify the nutritional deficiency and propose dietary interventions."
Digestion Walkthrough"Walk me through digestion of a meal of rice, chicken, and vegetables from mouth to large intestine. At each organ, pause and ask what I think happens before continuing."
NotebookLM (after upload)"Based on the uploaded lecture notes, what are the 10 most exam-likely concepts? Create 5 short-answer questions from them and quiz me one at a time."

BSc Biochemistry

Year 2 — Sem 2 · BIOC / CHEM / STAT
Enzyme Kinetics"Explain Michaelis-Menten kinetics to me in 3 stages: beginner, intermediate, then exam-level. After each stage, ask if I want to go deeper or be tested."
Metabolic Pathway Navigation"Teach me glycolysis step by step. For each step: name the enzyme, substrate, product, and significance. After 5 steps, quiz me on what you've taught."
Biomolecule Comparison"Create a comparison table of carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids. Compare: monomer units, bonds, functions, and examples of each."
Anki Flashcard Generation"Create 15 Anki-style flashcard pairs (Question / Answer) covering key TCA cycle enzymes — their substrates, products, and regulatory roles."

Day 3 — Practice Task

The AI Study Session

  • Choose one genuinely difficult topic from your courses
  • Ask Claude or ChatGPT to explain it three different ways (beginner / intermediate / exam-level)
  • Ask the AI to quiz you with 5 questions — answer before seeing feedback
  • Upload a lecture note to NotebookLM — ask it to generate 10 exam questions
  • Try one quiz platform (Quizizz or NotebookLM) — complete one study session
  • Write a 3-sentence summary of the topic entirely from memory, without notes
