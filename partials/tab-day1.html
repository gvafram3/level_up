The CWA Formula

KNUST CWA Formula
CWA (%) = Σ(Score × Credit Hours) ÷ Σ(Total Credit Hours)

Every course is weighted by its credit hours. A 3-credit course contributes 3× more to your CWA than a 1-credit course. A score below 40 (grade F) is a trail — it contributes 0 weighted points while still counting toward your total credit hours, actively dragging the average down.

Common Mistakes That Destroy CWAs

❌ Ignoring Credit Weight

Treating all courses as equal. A 3-credit trail is three times more damaging than a 1-credit trail. Always prioritise high-credit courses first.

❌ Not Tracking Continuously

Waiting for final results before worrying. By then, the maths is very hard. Calculate your CWA estimate after every exam.

❌ The "Next Semester" Trap

A CWA of 50% after Semester 1 requires near-perfect scores for 5+ semesters to reach First Class. The compound effect is brutal. Start now.

❌ Not Using the Simulator

Not modelling different scenarios. The projection tool shows exactly what average score you need next semester to hit your target. Use it.
