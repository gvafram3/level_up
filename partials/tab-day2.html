The Full Trail Picture

What Is a Trail?

A trail at KNUST is any F grade (score below 40%). Your weighted score contribution is 0 — contributing nothing to your weighted average while still counting toward your total credit hours, which divides down your CWA.

On Your Transcript

Both grades appear: the original F and the resit grade. Only the resit grade counts toward CWA. Postgraduate institutions and employers may see both. Clearing fast minimises visible damage.

The Compounding Effect

A trail in Semester 1 hurts more than a trail in Semester 6 — because it depresses your base CWA for all subsequent semesters. The earlier a trail is cleared, the more semesters your CWA has to recover.

Accumulation Risk

Accumulating trails without clearing them can lead to academic probation and eventually dismissal. KNUST requires a minimum CWA to remain in good academic standing. Know your programme's threshold.

Why Students Trail — Root Causes

🕐 Too Late, Too Little

Starting exam prep in the final week for a 3-credit science course is not preparation — it is hope. Consistent weekly study beats cramming every time.

📚 Passive Reading

Reading notes without testing understanding. If you cannot explain it, you have not learned it. Active recall (closing notes and writing what you remember) is far more effective.

🎯 No Past Questions

KNUST exams follow patterns. Past questions reveal which topics appear consistently, the question format, and the expected depth of answer. Not using them is leaving marks on the table.

📵 Social Displacement

Social media and hostel socialising are not problems in themselves — they become problems when they displace study time entirely. Time awareness is the skill.

🤷 Staying Confused

Sitting in confusion for weeks without seeking help. Use your lecturer, study groups, or AI tools (Day 3) to clarify concepts the moment you encounter them.

📋 No Syllabus Strategy

Studying randomly rather than topic by topic based on the syllabus. Map past questions to the syllabus — high-frequency topics should get more of your time.

Trail Recovery Strategy

Step 1 — Diagnose the Gap

If possible, collect your marked script. Where did you lose marks? Calculations? Definitions? Application? This tells you what to focus on, not the whole syllabus.

Step 2 — Past Questions First

Collect 3–5 years of past papers. Identify which topics appear every year. Build your resit plan around those topics first — they carry the most likely return on study time.

Step 3 — AI as Tutor

Use Claude or ChatGPT to explain every concept you're weak on — in plain language. Ask it to quiz you. Ask it to create questions that look like your past papers. (Full details on Day 3.)

Step 4 — Test, Don't Just Read

After each topic, close everything and write out what you know from memory. Check it. The act of retrieval builds the memory far more effectively than re-reading your notes.

💚

On the emotional side: Trailing a course does not define you — it is information, not identity. Many students who graduate with distinction have resit entries on their transcripts. What matters is how quickly and strategically you respond. If you're struggling emotionally, talk to someone. Isolation makes it worse.
