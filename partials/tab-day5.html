Past Questions Strategy

Step 1 — Collect and Sort

Get 3–5 years of past papers for each course. Sort by topic, not by year — you need to see which topics appear most frequently across exams.

Step 2 — Map to Syllabus

Mark which syllabus topics appear in past exams. Topics appearing every year are almost certain to appear again. Prioritise those with your study time.

Step 3 — Timed Practice

Do not just read past questions — answer them under exam conditions. Set a timer. Write out full answers. Then check or use AI to evaluate your response.

Step 4 — AI Evaluation

Prompt: "Here is my answer to this exam question. Evaluate it, tell me what I missed, and give me a mark out of 10 with reasons." This is powerful exam practice.

Study Timetable Rules
✅

Three Rules That Make a Timetable Work

Rule 1: No study block longer than 90 minutes before a break. Your brain consolidates memory during rest, not during study.
Rule 2: Hardest subjects go in your peak energy hours — typically morning for most people.
Rule 3: Build in two buffer days per week. Life will disrupt your plan. Buffer days ensure one disruption doesn't cascade into a week off track.
