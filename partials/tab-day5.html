Applying the Seminar — Week by Week

How to Use What You Learned

Information without application is just entertainment. Here is exactly how each day of this seminar translates into a specific action in the coming semester.

Day 1
→ Sem
CWA Tracking — Start from Week 1

Do not wait for results. After every test or assignment, update your score in the CWA simulator. Know your running average at all times. Set your target CWA before the semester begins — not after.

Day 2
→ Sem
Trail Prevention — Identify Risk Courses Early

In Week 2, look at your course list and identify the 1–2 courses most likely to give you trouble. Assign them extra study time immediately. Do not wait until Week 12 to realise you are in trouble.

Day 3
→ Sem
AI Study Habit — One Session Per Week Per Difficult Course

Pick one hard topic per week. Use Claude or ChatGPT to explain it, then quiz yourself. Upload your notes to NotebookLM at the start of each month. Use AI to study — not to submit.

Day 4
→ Sem
Word & Organisation — Set Up Your System Before Semester Starts

Create your KNUST folder structure this week. Name every file correctly from the first assignment. Set up cloud sync now. These habits take 30 minutes to set up and save hours every semester.

Past Questions Strategy

Step 1 — Collect and Sort

Get 3–5 years of past papers for each course. Sort by topic, not by year — you need to see which topics appear most frequently across exams.

Step 2 — Map to Syllabus

Mark which syllabus topics appear in past exams. Topics appearing every year are almost certain to appear again. Prioritise those with your study time.

Step 3 — Timed Practice

Do not just read past questions — answer them under exam conditions. Set a timer. Write out full answers. Then check or use AI to evaluate your response.

Step 4 — AI Evaluation

Prompt: "Here is my answer to this exam question. Evaluate it, tell me what I missed, and give me a mark out of 10 with reasons." This is powerful exam practice.

Study Timetable Rules
✅

Three Rules That Make a Timetable Work

Rule 1: No study block longer than 90 minutes before a break. Your brain consolidates memory during rest, not during study.
Rule 2: Hardest subjects go in your peak energy hours — typically morning for most people.
Rule 3: Build in two buffer days per week. Life will disrupt your plan. Buffer days ensure one disruption doesn't cascade into a week off track.
